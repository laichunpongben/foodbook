The honey that smells like the field it came from. Provence lavender honey is a *miel monofloral* — bees foraged predominantly on one species — and a reminder that honey is, fundamentally, a record of the local flora at the moment the bees collected it. The same hive in October would yield a different honey, darker and more herbaceous.
