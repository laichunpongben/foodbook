The northern Italian baked pasta in its DOP form, registered formally with the Italian Academy of Cuisine in 2003. Lasagne *verde* with ragù bolognese, besciamella, and parmigiano: six identifiable layers, no shortcuts. The recipe excludes garlic, ricotta, mozzarella, and red wine — all of which are common in southern and diaspora versions but explicitly not in the Bolognese.

Like most baked Italian pasta, this is project cooking — ragù day one, lasagne day two. The flavour cost of compressing it into a single day is real.
