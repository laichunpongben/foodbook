steps:
  - text: "Soak breadcrumbs in milk for 10 minutes. They'll absorb almost all the milk."
    durationSeconds: 600
  - text: "Sweat the diced onion in 1 tbsp butter over medium-low heat 6 minutes until translucent. Cool slightly."
    durationSeconds: 360
  - text: "Mix beef, pork, soaked breadcrumbs, cooked onion, egg, allspice, white pepper, and salt in a large bowl. Mix gently with your hands — overworking makes tough meatballs. Refrigerate 20 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 1200
  - text: "Shape into walnut-sized balls (about 25 g each). Wet your hands between batches to prevent sticking. You should get about 30 balls."
  - text: "Heat 2 tbsp butter in a wide pan over medium heat. Cook meatballs in batches, 10 minutes per batch, rolling them gently so all sides brown. Don't crowd — crowded meatballs steam, not brown."
    durationSeconds: 600
  - text: "Lift cooked meatballs to a plate. Keep warm."
  - text: "Make the gravy in the same pan. Melt 1 tbsp butter in the meatball drippings over medium heat. Sprinkle flour over and whisk 60 seconds — should turn into a pale roux."
  - text: "Pour in the beef stock, whisking. Bring to a simmer; let thicken 3 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 180
  - text: "Stir in cream, soy sauce, and Dijon. Simmer 2 minutes more. Taste — adjust salt."
  - text: "Return meatballs to the gravy. Warm through 3 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 180
  - text: "Plate with mashed potatoes, a generous spoon of lingonberry jam, and pickled cucumbers on the side."
notes: |
  Köttbullar are home food in Sweden — a Sunday roast equivalent, not a
  restaurant dish. The IKEA version is *not unlike* the Swedish home
  version, which is the same observation Swedes always make and slightly
  resent making.

  The lingonberry jam is structural to the plate, not optional. The
  sweet-acid tartness of the berries cuts through the rich cream gravy
  in exactly the way the dish needs.
