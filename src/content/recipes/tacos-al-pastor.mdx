steps:
  - text: "Toast the guajillos and anchos in a dry skillet over medium heat for 30 seconds per side until fragrant. Don't burn. Submerge in hot water for 20 minutes to rehydrate."
    durationSeconds: 1200
  - text: "Drain the chiles. In a blender, combine chiles, achiote, garlic, quartered onion, vinegar, citrus juices, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Blend to a smooth, thick marinade."
  - text: "Pour over the sliced pork. Massage so every piece is coated. Cover and refrigerate 12–24 hours."
    durationSeconds: 43200
  - text: "Heat the oven to 230°C / 450°F. Stack the marinated pork pieces on a foil-lined baking sheet, pressing down to form a single dense mound. Place a few onion wedges and pineapple slices around the meat."
  - text: "Roast 25 minutes. Turn the broiler on for the last 5 minutes for crust. The edges should char; the centre should be tender."
    durationSeconds: 1800
  - text: "While the meat rests, char the remaining pineapple slices on a hot dry skillet, 90 seconds per side. Dice."
    durationSeconds: 180
  - text: "Shave the pork into thin slices. Drop a handful onto two stacked warm tortillas. Top with charred pineapple, diced raw onion, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime."
notes: |
  The real al pastor cooks on a trompo — a vertical spit, learned from
  Lebanese shawarma cooks who emigrated to Mexico in the early 20th
  century — with a pineapple on top dripping juice into the meat. The
  home oven version skips the spit but keeps the marinade and the
  pineapple, which are the two flavours that make pastor pastor.

  Salsa: a quick green salsa of tomatillo, jalapeño, lime, salt is the
  classic side. Salsa roja (chipotle-based) works too. Skip the cheese
  and the lettuce — neither belongs.
