ingredients:
  - name: "Carnaroli rice"
    text: "320 g Carnaroli rice (or Vialone Nano)"
    growthNote: "Carnaroli holds its bite longer than Arborio — the Milanese choice. Don't substitute long-grain rice."
    group: "the rice"
  - name: "Saffron"
    text: "Generous pinch (0.4 g) saffron threads"
    from: "la-mancha-saffron"
    growthNote: "Bloomed in a ladle of hot stock for 10 minutes — the colour and flavour need time to release."
    group: "the rice"
  - name: "Beef bone marrow"
    text: "30 g beef bone marrow, finely diced (or 30 g butter if unavailable)"
    growthNote: "The signature Milanese ingredient — fat-rich, savoury, almost gelatinous."
    group: "the soffritto"
  - name: "Shallot"
    text: "1 small shallot, finely minced"
    group: "the soffritto"
  - name: "Dry white wine"
    text: "100 ml dry white wine"
    from: "burgundy-vineyard"
    group: "the soffritto"
  - name: "Beef stock"
    text: "1.2 L hot beef or chicken stock"
    growthNote: "Kept at a bare simmer in a separate pot. Cold stock shocks the rice."
    group: "the rice"
  - name: "Parmigiano Reggiano"
    text: "60 g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated"
    from: "emilia-parmigiano-dairy"
    group: "the mantecatura"
  - name: "Cold butter"
    text: "60 g cold butter, cubed"
    growthNote: "The mantecatura — the off-heat butter mount — gives the risotto its glossy, creamy finish."
    group: "the mantecatura"
  - name: "Sea salt"
    text: "Sea salt to taste"
    from: "trapani-salt-pans"
    group: "the rice"
steps:
  - text: "Bloom the saffron in a ladle of hot stock. Set aside."
    durationSeconds: 600
  - text: "In a wide heavy pan, melt the bone marrow over medium heat. Sweat the shallot 4 minutes until translucent — no colour."
    durationSeconds: 240
  - text: "Add the rice. Toast for 90 seconds, stirring, until the grains turn semi-translucent at the edges."
    durationSeconds: 90
  - text: "Pour in the wine. Stir until fully absorbed."
  - text: "Add hot stock one ladle at a time, stirring continuously. Wait until each ladle is mostly absorbed before adding the next. About 18 minutes total."
    durationSeconds: 1080
  - text: "After 10 minutes, stir in the bloomed saffron with its stock. The rice turns deep yellow."
  - text: "When the rice is al dente — biting through a grain shows a thin white core — pull off the heat. Adjust salt."
  - text: "Add the grated parmigiano and the cold butter cubes. Beat vigorously with a wooden spoon — *mantecare* — for 30 seconds. The risotto should be loose enough to flow on the plate (*all'onda*) but not soupy."
notes: |
  *All'onda* — wave-like — is the correct consistency. Tilt the plate;
  the risotto should slowly move. If it stays put, it's too thick;
  if it pools at the bottom, too thin.

  Pair traditionally with osso buco — the saffron risotto soaks up the
  marrow from the veal shank. The two dishes were almost certainly
  invented to be eaten together.
