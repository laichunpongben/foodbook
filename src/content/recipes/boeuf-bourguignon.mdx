steps:
  - text: "Marinate the beef in the wine with the bouquet garni, smashed garlic, and diced onion. 12–24 hours in the fridge. Turn once."
    durationSeconds: 43200
  - text: "Strain. Pat beef *very* dry on paper towels — wet beef won't brown. Reserve the wine and the onion."
  - text: "In a heavy dutch oven, render the lardons over medium heat 6 minutes. Lift out, reserve."
    durationSeconds: 360
  - text: "Brown the beef hard in the bacon fat, in three batches, all sides. Crowding steams; space browns. 4 minutes per batch."
    durationSeconds: 720
  - text: "Pour off most of the fat. Add the marinated onion and carrots. Stir 5 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 300
  - text: "Stir in tomato paste, cook 90 seconds. Add the cognac and let it bubble off, 30 seconds."
  - text: "Sprinkle flour over and stir 60 seconds. Pour in the reserved wine and beef stock. Add the bouquet garni and bring to a simmer."
  - text: "Return beef and lardons. Cover and braise at 160°C / 320°F for 2 hours, until the beef yields under a fork but holds its shape."
    durationSeconds: 7200
  - text: "While the beef braises, prepare the garnish. Melt half the butter in a wide pan over medium heat. Add pearl onions with a pinch of salt and 1 tsp sugar. Cook 12 minutes until glazed amber. Lift out."
    durationSeconds: 720
  - text: "In the same pan, melt the remaining butter and brown the mushrooms hard — 6 minutes, single layer, don't stir until they release. Season."
    durationSeconds: 360
  - text: "When the beef is done, lift it out. Strain the sauce, return to the dutch oven, and reduce over medium-high heat until it coats a spoon, 8 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 480
  - text: "Return the beef, the lardons, the glazed onions, the mushrooms. Warm through. Taste, adjust salt and pepper."
notes: |
  Boeuf bourguignon and coq au vin are siblings — same Burgundian
  braising technique, same wine, same garnish trinity (lardons,
  pearl onions, mushrooms), different animal. If you've made coq au
  vin once, you can make bourguignon blindfolded.

  Make a day ahead. The flavour gain from overnight is real. Reheat
  gently, never boil — boiling toughens the beef.
