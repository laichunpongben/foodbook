ingredients:
  - { text: "1.2 kg ripe plum tomatoes", from: "example-producer", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "2 medium onions, finely diced", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "3 cloves garlic, minced", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "2 stalks celery, finely diced", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "60 ml extra-virgin olive oil", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "1 tsp fine sea salt, plus more to taste", group: "for the sauce" }
  - { text: "1 small bunch basil, stems reserved", group: "to finish" }
  - { text: "Black pepper", group: "to finish" }
steps:
  - text: "Blister tomatoes in batches in a wide pan over high heat until skins split, ~2 min per batch. Crush with a wooden spoon. Set aside."
    durationSeconds: 600
  - text: "Sweat onion, garlic, and celery in olive oil over low heat until translucent, no color. 12–15 minutes."
    durationSeconds: 900
  - text: "Add crushed tomatoes, salt, and basil stems. Bring to a bare simmer."
  - text: "Cook, partially covered, at the lowest possible heat for 5–6 hours. Stir every 30 minutes. The sauce is done when a wooden spoon leaves a clean trail through the bottom of the pan for two seconds."
    durationSeconds: 21600
  - text: "Fish out basil stems. Off-heat, tear in fresh basil leaves and crack black pepper. Adjust salt."
notes: |
  Hold-over notes: cools and keeps three days refrigerated, six months frozen.
  Doubles cleanly. Halves cleanly. Will not scale to 6× — the surface area
  needed for evaporation outpaces the largest pot a home kitchen can hold.
