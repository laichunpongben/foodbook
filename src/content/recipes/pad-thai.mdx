ingredients:
  - name: "Rice noodles"
    text: "180 g flat rice noodles, 5 mm wide (sen lek)"
    growthNote: "Dried, then soaked — never boiled. Boiling makes them gluey before they hit the wok."
    group: "the noodles"
  - name: "Tamarind paste"
    text: "3 tbsp tamarind paste (or 4 tbsp tamarind concentrate)"
    growthNote: "Real tamarind, not the bottled brown sauce labelled 'pad thai sauce' — that's mostly ketchup and corn syrup."
    group: "the sauce"
  - name: "Fish sauce"
    text: "3 tbsp fish sauce"
    group: "the sauce"
  - name: "Palm sugar"
    text: "3 tbsp palm sugar, grated (or light brown sugar)"
    group: "the sauce"
  - name: "Shrimp"
    text: "200 g raw shrimp, peeled (or 200 g firm tofu, cubed)"
    group: "the protein"
  - name: "Eggs"
    text: "2 large eggs"
    group: "the protein"
  - name: "Pressed tofu"
    text: "60 g pressed firm tofu, diced small"
    group: "the protein"
  - name: "Dried shrimp"
    text: "1 tbsp dried shrimp, chopped (optional but traditional)"
    group: "the protein"
  - name: "Preserved radish"
    text: "1 tbsp sweet preserved radish (chai po wan), chopped"
    growthNote: "Adds a fermented salty-sweet note. Find it in Asian groceries; not optional in a real pad thai."
    group: "the sauce"
  - name: "Garlic"
    text: "3 cloves garlic, minced"
    heroUrl: "https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Garlic_bulbs.jpg?width=1280"
    from: "tropea-allium-farm"
    group: "the aromatics"
  - name: "Shallots"
    text: "3 shallots, finely sliced"
    group: "the aromatics"
  - name: "Bean sprouts"
    text: "150 g fresh bean sprouts"
    group: "the finish"
  - name: "Chinese chives"
    text: "Small bunch garlic chives (or scallions), cut into 4 cm batons"
    group: "the finish"
  - name: "Roasted peanuts"
    text: "40 g roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed"
    group: "the finish"
  - name: "Lime"
    text: "2 limes, halved"
    group: "the finish"
  - name: "Chili flakes"
    text: "Dried chili flakes, to taste"
    group: "the finish"
  - name: "Neutral oil"
    text: "3 tbsp neutral oil"
    group: "the aromatics"
steps:
  - text: "Soak the rice noodles in warm tap water for 45 minutes until pliable but not soft. They'll finish cooking in the wok."
    durationSeconds: 2700
  - text: "Whisk the tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar in a bowl until the sugar dissolves. Taste — it should be aggressively sour-salty-sweet."
  - text: "Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wok over high heat until smoking. Add half the shallots, garlic, dried shrimp, and pressed tofu. Stir-fry 60 seconds."
    durationSeconds: 60
  - text: "Add the shrimp. Cook 90 seconds until just pink. Push to the side of the wok."
    durationSeconds: 90
  - text: "Add 1 tbsp oil to the cleared space. Crack in the eggs. Scramble loosely until set but still soft, 30 seconds."
    durationSeconds: 30
  - text: "Drain the noodles. Add to the wok with the preserved radish and the sauce. Toss to coat — 2 minutes of constant motion."
    durationSeconds: 120
  - text: "When the noodles are glossy and have absorbed most of the sauce, add three-quarters of the bean sprouts, the chives, and half the crushed peanuts. Toss 30 seconds — the sprouts should stay crunchy."
    durationSeconds: 30
  - text: "Plate. Top with remaining bean sprouts, peanuts, and a lime half on the side. Chili flakes on the table."
notes: |
  Cook one or two portions at a time. A home stove cannot generate the
  heat needed for four portions in a single wok — you get steamed
  noodles, not stir-fried noodles. Cook in sequence and plate as you go.

  The traditional accompaniment is a small dish with lime wedges,
  chili flakes, fish sauce, and palm sugar — the diner balances the
  bowl to their own preference. Pad thai is not a finished dish; it's
  a base.
